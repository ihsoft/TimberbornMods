# DualDistrictStorage Workshop Slideshow Instructions

These instructions apply to promotional images under `DualDistrictStorage/workshop/Slideshow/`. Follow the repository
Promotional Visual Operational Knowledge and release authorization rules as applicable.

## Current Outputs And Composition

`thumbnail-master.png` is the 1672x941 review master. `thumbnail.jpg` is the 1280x720 platform delivery artifact. Review
both after every visual change; the delivery JPEG is the final evidence for scale, alignment, legibility, and compression.

The established composition contains:

- the title `DUAL DISTRICT STORAGE`;
- the subtitle `TWO DISTRICTS • ONE SHARED STOCKPILE`;
- `DISTRICT A` and `DISTRICT B` labels aligned with their actual district-center subjects;
- the warehouse, tank, and pile readable along the central district boundary;
- the dashed boundary line passing through the shared storage buildings;
- uniform map readability without a vignette or edge darkening.

When the request is to replace or reframe the gameplay screenshot, preserve those elements unless the user explicitly
approves a redesign. Frame the meaningful building group within the useful region below the title and subtitle. Treat
empty screenshot margins as editing latitude rather than content that must remain, and validate label alignment against
the visible district centers rather than symmetric canvas coordinates.

## Current Reproducibility Boundary

The tracked master and JPEG are currently flattened outputs. The repository does not yet own an independent raw
gameplay source, transparent typography/annotation overlay, layered editable source, or durable deterministic
compositor for this composition.

Do not treat an ignored temporary screenshot or compositor as the owner, and do not use the changing flattened master
as a self-referential transformation source that can compound edits. Before a future layer-only replacement, report
this limitation and establish whether the task should add durable inputs or accept another bounded manual edit.

If a reproducible workflow is added later, record the immutable input identities, overlay or layered-source owner,
framing parameters, compositor, and exact master/delivery outputs here. Do not document a temporary recovery script as
that workflow.
